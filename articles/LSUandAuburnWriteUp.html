Bulldogs with a Dominant Performance in Home Opener


The Louisiana Tech Wrestling Club continued the momentum from their season opening tri-meet victory at Lyon College with a dominant performance in their home opener at the Lambright Sports and Wellness Center against the LSU Tigers and the Auburn University Tigers, both club programs participating in the NCWA. LSU, an in-state opponent for the bulldogs, finished 8th last season at the NCWA Southwest Conference Championships (7.5 points ahead of the Bulldogs 11th place finish).

Auburn Dual (Louisiana Tech 31 - Auburn University 4)


Beginning the day against Auburn University, the Bulldogs looked to continue their strong start to the season. The Bulldogs double-forfeited the 125, 133, and 141 pound weight classes. Auburn's 141 pound wrestler bumped up to 149 pounds to take on Tech's Samuel Favaza, who, after surrendering the first takedown, scored 8 unanswered points to secure a commanding 8-3 victory for the Bulldogs. At 157 pounds, Sean Bell faced a tough opponent, and ultimately fell by major decision, but showed great resilience throughout the match. Larry Davis continued his undefeated season accepting a forfeit at 165 pounds, as did Micah Thompson at 174 pounds and Raiden Flores at 184 pounds. Raymond Favaza at 197 pounds secured a dominant 14-1 major decision over his Auburn opponent. Finally, Noah Lucas at 235 pounds finished the dual with a first period pin, giving the Bulldogs a dominant 31-4 victory over Auburn University.

LSU Dual (Louisiana Tech 28 - LSU 15)


Following the Auburn dual, the Bulldogs faced off against in-state rival LSU. The Tigers and Bulldogs double-forfeited the 125 and 141 pound weight classes. At 133 pounds, LSU accepted a forfeit. Samuel Favaza at 149 pounds continued his undefeated season with a forfeit. Sean Bell showed the home crowd why the match doesn't end until the final whistle, scoring a last second takedown to secure a thrilling 13-2 major decision victory. Larry Davis continued his dominant start to the season with a first period pin at 165 pounds. Micah Thompson at 174 pounds wrestled a tough returning national qualifier from LSU, ultimately falling 4-0. Raiden Flores started slow at 184 pounds, securing a "come-from-behind" first period pin. Raymond Favaza collected a forfeit at 197 pounds, and Noah Lucas finished the dual, stepping up to 235 pounds to face LSU's returning All-American, to whom he ultimately fell via pin.
